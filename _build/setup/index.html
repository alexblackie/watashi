My Setup

I always love reading about how other people use their computers and desks. Sites like UsesThis and Simple Desks (R.I.P.) have taken many hours of my life, and I have taken inspiration from them many times. This page will serve as a perpetually-updated reference for how I'm currently using my computer.

Hardware

My current “Daily Driver” computer is the Dell XPS 13 (9380). It's plenty fast, thin and light, has a fantastic screen, great WiFi performance, and a decently comfortable keyboard. High-DPI under Linux is still hit-and-miss, but the laptop itself is probably one of the best computers I've owned.

Primarily, I use 2x Dell U2415 24" 1200p Monitors (on a dual monitor arm), a Varmilo Sakura TKL mechanical keyboard with Cherry MX Brown switches, and a Logitech MX Master (V1) mouse. Since video calls are a frequent occurrence, I have a Logitech C920 webcam and a Blue Yeti “Blackout Edition” microphone, shock-mounted to a Blue mic boom. Everything is plugged into a CalDigit TS3+ Thunderbolt Dock which allows me to live the "one cable" lifestyle, and I love it.

For work, I have a standard-issue 15" Macbook Pro (2019), which plugs into the same Thunderbolt dock (the future is here). I also swap out the MX Master for an Apple Magic Trackpad because mouse support in macOS is insufferable.

Software

Probably the most accurate and detailed information about my software setup is my dotfiles and the Ansible playbooks that I use to provision my workstations.

For operating systems, I tend to stick to the latest Fedora Workstation release. For my work-provided Macbook I am of course stuck with macOS, unfortunately. For non-desktop machines, CentOS is my server OS of choice, though Kubernetes has made that less important in recent years.

My desktop environment of choice has been GNOME for many years, and I run it nearly stock, with only two extensions enabled. I spend a significant amount of my time in Firefox and vim. For larger projects, especially where IDE-ish features become useful, I tend to use Visual Studio Code, almost exclusively for Intellisense and the side-by-side markdown preview mode.

I exclusively use a local music library, all of which I have purchased and built up over many years. On macOS I'm still unfortunately stuck using iTunes Music.app, which is very slow, clunky, and buggy. On Linux, I use Lollypop which works very well and is a model for how music library apps should be done.
