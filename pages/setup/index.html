I always love reading about how other people use their computers and desks. Sites like UsesThis and Simple Desks (R.I.P.) have taken many hours of my life, and I have taken inspiration from them many times. This page will serve as a perpetually-updated reference for how I'm currently using my computer.

Hardware

After several years and various laptops and operating systems, I have finally landed on a maxed-out Dell XPS 13 (9380). It's plenty fast, thin and light, has a fantastic screen, great WiFi performance, and a rather comfortable keyboard with keys that actually move. It's a fair departure from my Thinkpad lineage, but I quite like it.

At home, I use 2x Dell U2415 24" 1200p Monitors (on a dual monitor arm), a Filco Majestouch 2 Ninja mechanical keyboard with Cherry MX Brown switches and an aftermarket set of PBT keycaps, and a Logitech MX Master (V1) mouse. Everything is plugged into a CalDigit TS3+ Thunderbolt Dock which allows me to live the "one cable" lifestyle, and I love it.

At work, I have a standard-issue Macbook Pro, a standard-issue LG 4K monitor, a Varmilo Sakura TKL mechanical keyboard (also with Cherry MX Brown switches), and an Apple Magic Trackpad.

Software

Probably the most accurate and detailed information about my software setup is my dotfiles and the Ansible playbooks that I use to provision my workstations.

For operating systems, I tend to stick to the latest Fedora Workstation release or I may also use the latest Ubuntu Desktop LTS depending on my mood or the machine's role. Of course, on my work Macbook I am stuck with macOS, unfortunately.

I spend a significant amount of time in Firefox and vim. My shell of choice is zsh though for many years I used and still have a strong respect for bash. Depending on my mood, I might use Sublime Text 3, which continues to be an excellent text editor.

I exclusively use a local music library, all of which I have purchased and built up over many years. On macOS I'm still unfortunately stuck using iTunes Music.appbecause apparently no one has made one decent music library application for that platform. On Linux, I use Lollypop which works very well though I wish they would stop adding features.
