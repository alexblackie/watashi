Sometimes you find an album that doesn't have a single bad song on it. The albums here represent my collection of those exemplifying true musicianship and emotion, with a staying power that lasts years. I've sorted them roughly chronologically.

While I mostly focus on albums for this list, all the artists mentioned here I generally recommend as well; these albums are simply the ones that really stand out to me.

Favourite Worst Nightmare

Arctic Monkeys

2007 · Rock

Arctic Monkeys' second album really hits all the right marks in terms of evolution of sound and production, paired with superb songwriting with songs that get stuck in your head for days. While I still enjoy the later works of the band, Favourite Worst Nightmare maintains the original harder rock sound that I fell in love with. I've listened to it since its release back in 2007, and I have never tired of it, and still hold it as one of the best albums of all time.

トライアングル

Perfume

2009 · Electropop

「トライアングル」or "⊿" or "Triangle" is the third album from Japanese pop group Perfume, and stands out to me as one of Nakata's most consistent works. While I find a lot of their albums have some hits and some misses, Triangle is a consistent hit for me. (That said, Level3 and Future Pop both are both very close to being on this list.)

Warp Riders

The Sword

2010 · Metal

"Warp Riders" is a delightful sci-fi metal concept album. The old-school metal sound, space themes, and excellent songwriting all contribute to this album's grandure. Something about the warm tones and strong riffs make this album comfortable and persistent.

Death Came Through a Phantom Ship

Carach Angren

2010 · Black Metal

"Death Came Through a Phantom Ship" was my introduction to Canach Angren, and still remains one of my favourite albums of theirs. The concept album follows the decreasing sanity of an ship captain, with plenty of horrific events throughout. With their masterful musicianship and symphonic accompaniments, Carach Angren is one of black metal's greatest.

Where the Corpses Sink Forever

Carach Angren

2012 · Black Metal

Following "Death Came Through a Phantom Ship" is this album, which is one that I continue to come back to, and always listen from front-to-back every time. The album follows a harrowing tale of a soldier's nightmarish suffering, and is full of desparation and frustration. 10/10.

Siberia

Lights

2012 · Electronic

Canadian electronic artist Lights has been consistently in my library since high school, and her work continues to impress to this day. "Sibera" was Lights' followup to her breakout 2009 debut, and I find it fascinating how it simultaneously maintains aspects of "The Listening" while bringing more modern production and confident experimentation. I always come back to this album and am reminded how much I enjoy it.

The Parallax II: Future Sequence

Between the Buried and Me

2012 · Metal

BTBAM continues to produce some of my favourite music of all time, however their 2012 release "The Parallax II: Future Sequence" has specifically had an enormous impact on my music listening over the years, and is a truly phenomenal work.

Additionally, there is a live studio performance of the band playing this album front-to-back which I always keep closeby and often rewatch. I have also had the pleasure of seeing them live on a couple different occasions and theirs were probably the best shows I have ever attended.

Rivers of Nihil

Metal

Every album from Rivers of Nihil deserves a place in this list. "The Conscious Seed of Light" is a wall-to-wall powerhouse of an album; "Monarchy" is a huge leap in production, putting polish on the same great songwriting ability and musicianship; and finally "Where Owls Know Your Name" takes the band to the next level, incorporating elements of jazz into the same heavy and atmospheric sound. Rivers of Nihil remain one of my all-time favourite bands, and are another group who deliver a fantastic live performance.

Another Eternity

Purity Ring

2015 · Electronic

This electropop duo—and fellow Canadians—have been in my heavy rotation for years. Every album is unique in its own way, each communicating a plethora of emotion in every song. "Another Eternity" was my introduction to the group, but the entire discography has become some of my favourite electronic music of all time. With time, their newer release "Womb" will likely be on this list as well.

Apex

Unleash the Archers

2017 · Metal

Not only was Unleash the Archers born in the same city as I, they also put out some excellent power metal. "Apex" hits on all the right notes of fantasy storytelling, powerful musicianship, and mind-invading songwriting -- it's another that I always put on and listen to in one sitting, and often its songs go through my head for days at a time.
